Now let's add a second technique. This one is about reality checking — training your brain to question whether what's happening is real or a dream.

...

In waking life, we almost never question whether we're dreaming. Everything feels real, so we assume it is. But in dreams, things feel real too — that's why we don't realize we're dreaming. The dream feels completely normal, even when impossible things are happening.

...

Reality checks are small habits you can build that carry over into dreams. Things like looking at your hands, or checking a clock, or trying to push a finger through your palm. In waking life, these things behave normally — your hands look normal, clocks show the right time, your finger bounces off your palm. But in dreams, they behave differently. Text changes when you look away and look back. Your hands might have six fingers, or they might be blurry, or they might be the wrong size. Clocks show impossible numbers, or the time changes every time you look.

...

Right now, in bed, we're going to practice a simple awareness check. Not because you're dreaming right now — you're not — but because practicing this while awake trains your brain to do it while asleep. It builds the habit. And habits carry over into dreams.
