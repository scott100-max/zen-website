CBT Exercises

Change How You Think, Change How You Feel

Guided exercises built on the principles of Cognitive Behavioural Therapy — the most widely researched and clinically supported approach to managing difficult thoughts and emotions. Close your eyes, press play, and let the session guide you through it.

Self-help tools, not therapy. These sessions are CBT-informed guided exercises designed to support your wellbeing. They are not a replacement for professional mental health support. If you are experiencing significant distress, please reach out to a qualified therapist or your GP.

What Is CBT?

Cognitive Behavioural Therapy is based on a simple but powerful idea: the way we think about a situation affects how we feel about it — and how we respond to it. By noticing unhelpful thought patterns and gently examining them, we can shift both our emotional state and our behaviour. CBT is the most evidence-based psychological approach in modern practice, recommended by the NHS, NICE, and major clinical bodies worldwide.

Three Pillars

Cognitive Restructuring Identify and challenge distorted or unhelpful thought patterns — replacing them with more balanced perspectives.
Thought Defusion Create distance between you and your thoughts. Observe them without being swept away by them.
Behavioural Activation Break the inertia of low mood by re-engaging with activities that bring purpose, pleasure, or connection.

How a CBT Exercise Works

Each session follows a structured arc designed to guide you through the process

01 Ground Settle into stillness. A short breathing exercise brings you into the present moment and prepares your mind for the work ahead.
02 Notice Identify the thought, belief, or pattern you're working with. No judgement — just clear-eyed observation of what's happening in your mind.
03 Examine Apply the CBT technique: challenge the evidence, defuse from the thought, or explore an alternative perspective. This is where the shift happens.
04 Integrate Consolidate what you've noticed. Anchor the new perspective. Return to your day carrying something different from when you arrived.
Evidence-Based Every exercise is grounded in established CBT principles recommended by NICE and the NHS. No improvisation — just proven techniques.
Guided, Not Lectured These aren't audiobooks about CBT. They're immersive guided exercises — close your eyes, follow the voice, and do the work in real time.
