ASMR

Immersive audio environments to help you relax, focus, or drift off to sleep.

What is ASMR?

ASMR (Autonomous Sensory Meridian Response) is that gentle, tingling sensation you feel when you hear certain sounds — rain on a window, leaves rustling, a crackling fire. It's deeply relaxing and can help quiet an overactive mind.

Our soundscapes are carefully curated to help you relax, focus, or drift off to sleep. Use them on their own or layer them with our guided meditations.

Let's build this together — if there's a sound you'd love to hear, tell us.

The History & Science of ASMR

Understanding why these sounds work.

A Brief History

People have experienced ASMR sensations for centuries, but the term itself was only coined in 2010 by Jennifer Allen in an online forum. What started as a niche internet community quickly grew into a global phenomenon.

By 2015, ASMR had millions of dedicated followers on YouTube, and researchers began taking notice. Today, it's recognised as a legitimate relaxation tool used by millions worldwide for sleep, stress relief, and focus.

Why It Works

Research from the University of Sheffield (2018) found that ASMR significantly reduces heart rate and increases positive emotions. The tingles you feel are linked to the release of oxytocin and endorphins — the same chemicals associated with bonding and relaxation.

Brain imaging studies show that ASMR activates regions associated with emotional arousal and social cognition, similar to the brain patterns seen during meditation. It's not just pleasant — it's physiologically calming.
