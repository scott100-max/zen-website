Week 2

The Slow Train North

The platform is almost empty. A single lamp throws a circle of amber light across the wet concrete. Beyond it, everything is dark and still.

The train arrives without hurry. A low rumble first, then the slow appearance of a headlamp through the mist. It pulls in gently, as though it does not wish to disturb the quiet.

You step aboard. The carriage is warm and half-lit. Old seats in faded blue, a window already misted at the edges. You choose a seat by the glass and settle in.

The door closes. A whistle sounds, distant and thin. And with a soft lurch, the train begins to move.

There is nowhere you need to be. No arrival time that matters. Just the slow, steady rhythm of wheels on track, carrying you north through the dark.
