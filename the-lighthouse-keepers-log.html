Week 4

The Lighthouse Keeper's Log

The lighthouse stands at the end of a causeway that disappears twice a day beneath the tide. Tonight, the water is high and the path is gone. You are here, and the sea is everywhere else.

The door is heavy. Iron-studded oak, swollen with salt air. It closes behind you with a sound like a full stop at the end of a long sentence.

Inside, a spiral staircase climbs the full height of the tower. The walls are whitewashed stone, two feet thick, and they hold the cold of the Atlantic in them like a memory.

You climb. The steps are worn smooth in the centre where a century of boots have passed. At the top, a small round room with windows on every side.

The sea stretches out in all directions. Dark and restless and vast. And somewhere below this room, somewhere deep in the walls, the lighthouse hums — a low, steady vibration that you feel in your chest more than hear.
